Shakshuka Recipe

Description

Shakshuka — A Fiery Skillet of Comfort

Shakshuka is a celebration of bold flavors and rustic simplicity. Originating from North Africa and widely embraced in Middle Eastern kitchens, this dish features perfectly poached eggs nestled in a bubbling skillet of spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce. With every spoonful, you get the comforting tang of tomatoes, the smoky sweetness of peppers, and the gentle heat of paprika and cumin — all brought together with the rich creaminess of soft-cooked eggs. It’s a dish that demands warm bread for scooping, lively conversation around the table, and a few moments of silence just to savor the harmony of flavors. Shakshuka is humble yet dramatic, and always a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • Salt, pepper, chili flakes
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro

Steps

  1. Sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper until soft.
  2. Add tomatoes and spices. Simmer 10 minutes.
  3. Make wells in the sauce and crack in the eggs.
  4. Cover and cook 5–8 minutes (until eggs are set).
  5. Garnish and serve with warm bread
